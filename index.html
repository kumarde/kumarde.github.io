I am a postdoctoral researcher at Stanford University. My research takes a data-driven approach to studying computer security and privacy behaviors on the Internet.

Most recently, I have been focused on understanding security and safety threats that emerge at the interface between technology and society, like online abuse and misinformation. I conduct large-scale empirical measurements to study how these threats are operationalized and leverage these insights to design defenses that can better protect Internet users. You can find my publications here, my CV here, and my Google Scholar page here.

I won the 2020 CRA/CCC CI Fellowship and the 2022 David and Helen Gurley Brown Institute Magic Grant in support of my research into online abuse.

I am on the job market this year! I am interested in tenure-track academic positions or research/data scientist positions in industry. Reach out at kumarde@cs.stanford.edu if you are hiring!
